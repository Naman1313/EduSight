ASER 2022 REPORT - KEY FINDINGS AND INTERVENTION GUIDELINES

SECTION 1: ATTENDANCE AND DROPOUT RISK FACTORS

Children who miss more than 10 days of school in a month are at high risk of permanent dropout. 
Seasonal absence is the leading cause of dropout in rural India, particularly during harvest seasons in October-November and March-April.
Students who miss more than 5 consecutive days show a pattern that often leads to permanent dropout within 60-90 days.
Girls in Class 7-8 are particularly vulnerable to dropout due to household responsibilities during agricultural seasons.
First generation learners are 3x more likely to drop out when they fall behind in foundational subjects.

SECTION 2: ACADEMIC PERFORMANCE AND DROPOUT CORRELATION

Students scoring below 40% in Mathematics are 4x more likely to drop out within the academic year.
Students failing both Mathematics and Science simultaneously have a dropout probability of over 70%.
Grade repetition increases dropout risk by 2.5x compared to promoted students.
Students who fall more than one grade level behind in reading are unlikely to recover without intervention.
Mid-term performance is a stronger predictor of dropout than annual exam results.

SECTION 3: PRATHAM INTERVENTION STRATEGIES

HOME VISIT PROTOCOL:
- Conduct home visits within 48 hours of identifying a high-risk student
- Meet both parents or guardians if possible
- Discuss specific academic concerns without blame
- Create a flexible attendance plan during harvest season
- Follow up within 2 weeks to assess improvement

REMEDIAL EDUCATION PROGRAM:
- Enroll at-risk students in Pratham's Teaching at the Right Level (TaRL) program
- TaRL focuses on foundational literacy and numeracy skills
- Students attend 2-hour remedial sessions 3 times per week
- Program has shown 40% improvement in at-risk student retention
- Peer learning groups of 4-5 students improve outcomes significantly

PARENT ENGAGEMENT:
- Monthly parent meetings increase attendance by 25%
- Share student progress reports in simple visual format
- Involve parents in school management committees
- Provide parents with simple home learning activities
- Address economic barriers through scholarship linkage

SECTION 4: SEASONAL INTERVENTION GUIDELINES

HARVEST SEASON (October - November):
- Allow flexible attendance during peak harvest period
- Provide catch-up classes after harvest season ends
- Connect families to MGNREGA work opportunities to reduce child labor
- Coordinate with local panchayat for awareness campaigns

SOWING SEASON (June - July):
- Monitor attendance closely at start of academic year
- Conduct enrollment drives in collaboration with ASHA workers
- Provide free uniforms and books immediately to reduce dropout

SECTION 5: SCHOOL MANAGEMENT COMMITTEE ACTIONS

Block Education Officers should:
- Review attendance data weekly for schools with high dropout history
- Conduct monthly school visits during high-risk periods
- Coordinate with NGO partners for ground-level intervention
- Report persistent cases to District Education Officer within 30 days
- Maintain intervention logs for each at-risk student

NGO Caseworker Protocol:
- Visit at-risk student homes within 72 hours of flagging
- Document household economic situation
- Connect families to government welfare schemes (PM-POSHAN, scholarship programs)
- Provide monthly follow-up for 3 months after intervention

SECTION 6: GOVERNMENT SCHEME LINKAGES

PM-POSHAN (Mid Day Meal Scheme):
- Ensures daily nutrition for enrolled students
- Attendance incentive - students more likely to attend for meals
- Check enrollment status for all high-risk students

Scholarship Programs:
- National Means-cum-Merit Scholarship for Class 8 students
- State government scholarships for SC/ST students
- Pre-matric scholarship for minority students
- Connect at-risk families to nearest scholarship registration center

SECTION 7: EVIDENCE-BASED OUTCOMES

Interventions that work:
- Home visits within 48 hours: 65% reduction in dropout rate
- Remedial education enrollment: 40% improvement in retention
- Parent engagement programs: 25% improvement in attendance
- Flexible attendance during harvest: 50% reduction in seasonal dropout
- Peer learning groups: 35% improvement in academic performance

Interventions to avoid:
- Punitive measures for absence increase dropout risk
- Shaming students publicly reduces engagement
- Involving police or legal threats causes permanent dropout
- Demanding immediate attendance without understanding context

SECTION 8: DISTRICT-SPECIFIC RISK FACTORS IN UTTAR PRADESH

Agricultural dependency affects 60% of families in rural UP
Average household income below poverty line: 35% of government school students
First generation learners: 45% of government school enrollment
Distance to school greater than 3km: 20% of students
Lack of toilet facilities: major factor in girl dropout at puberty
